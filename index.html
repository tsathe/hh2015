SHARING MORE THAN A ZIP CODE

The Annual Hunger and Homelessness Auction was established in 1993 by Yale health professional students interested in raising community awareness of and alleviating the adversity caused by hunger and homelessness in the Greater New Haven area. Our mission is to educate the community on key social issues: poverty and food security to affordable housing, unemployment, health care equality, and the implications of substance abuse and mental illness.

Christian Community Action

https://ccahelping.org

For almost 50 years, Christian Community Action (CCA) has been combatting homelessness in New Haven’s Hill district by providing emergency housing, crisis intervention, and diversion assistance. The grant from H&H will enable CCA to expand their Neighborhood Assistance Program to provide diversion assistance to an additional 30 individuals in the coming year.

Liberty Community Services

http://www.libertycs.org

Liberty Community Services (LCS) strives to end homelessness in New Haven by helping people achieve and maintain permanent housing within the community. In particular, LCS provides assistance to people suffering from HIV/AIDS, mental illness, and addiction. This year’s H&H grant will support outreach efforts to identify and rapidly address the most pressing individuals needs of homeless people throughout New Haven.

Continuum of Care

http://www.continuumct.com

Continuum of Care provides comprehensive community-based resident and support services for individuals with psychiatric and developmental disabilities. Through their Crisis Stabilization program, they divert individuals, many of whom are chronically homeless, from psychiatric emergency departments and in-patient facilities in order to provide a more direct path towards reintegration into the community. The grant from H&H will fund a 1-year pilot Peer Bridger program that will employ a formerly homeless individual to serve as an advocate and guide for currently homeless individuals to assist their recovery and journey back to community life.

Community Healthcare Van

http://www.yccr.yale.edu/places/chcv.aspx

For the past 22 years, the Community Health Care Van (CHCV) has provided much needed outreach, prevention, and medical care to the most vulnerable of New Haven’s residents. As a virtual “patient-centered medical home” within a 40-foot van, the CHCV’s mobile medical clinic (MMC) targets high risk neighborhoods impacted by poverty, drug use and unemployment to provide medical and psychiatric services, and diagnosis and treatment for infectious diseases and substance use disorders. This year’s grant from H&H will go towards medical supplies and education materials that are outside the scope of CHCV’s federal grant support.

Integrated Refugee & Immigrant Services

http://www.irisct.org

Integrated Refugee & Immigrant Services (IRIS) resettles approximately 225 refugees, coming from numerous countries including Afghanistan, Iraq, Syria, Sudan, Ethiopia, and Democratic Republic of Congo, within the Greater New Haven area. IRIS meets the basic daily needs of all refugees they resettle and also promotes integration and self-sufficiency through educational and social services. With this year’s grant from H&H, IRIS will develop a Health and Wellness program for refugee women focusing on Psychoeducation, an orientation to available mental-health resources, a women’s empowerment group, an English class, and a cross-cultural mentorship program.

FISH of Greater New Haven

http://fishofgreaternewhaven.org

FISH of Greater New Haven delivers free groceries to food-insecure individuals who are homebound, many of whom are elderly, handicapped, or have recently undergone major operations. They are the only organization in the area doing this work. This year’s grant from H&H will enable FISH to serve 20% more individuals than in the previous year, helping to meet the tremendous demand for their services.
